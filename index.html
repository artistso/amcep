02 / DECISIVE FAILURE

The source formula rewards contradiction in its intended regime.

Sₙ = (ρ + T − ρxⁿ) / E   ⇒   ∂Sₙ/∂ρ = (1 − xⁿ) / E

For E > 0, 0 ≤ x < 1, and n > 0, the derivative is positive. Raising ρ raises the score. This is the opposite of the stated contradiction-penalty principle.

Verdict: the original monotonicity claim is falsified. The equation remains available as a control and historical artifact.

A repaired sign is necessary, but not sufficient.

Cumulative penalty

Sᶜₙ = (T − λρ(1 − |x|ⁿ)) / E

Non-increasing in ρ for E > 0, λ ≥ 0, and |x| ≤ 1. For |x| < 1, the limiting penalty remains λρ/E.

Conditionally coherent

05 / REAL-WORLD GATES

Every promoted principle must pass independent rejection gates.

  1. Semantic: variables map to observable quantities without circular definitions.
  2. Dimensional: added and subtracted quantities have compatible units or explicit nondimensionalization.
  3. Mathematical: domains, invariants, limits, counterexamples, and boundary behavior are explicit.
  4. Computational: unit, property, adversarial, and independent implementation tests agree.
  5. Formal: Lean checks exact propositions over exact types without placeholders or convenience axioms.
  6. Empirical: a locked protocol, simple baselines, raw artifacts, and uncertainty can reject the prediction.
  7. Replication: an independent implementation reproduces the scoped result.
