02 / FIRST FAILURE

The source formula rewards contradiction in its stated regime.

Sₙ = (ρ + T − ρxⁿ) / E    ⇒    ∂Sₙ/∂ρ = (1 − xⁿ) / E

For E > 0, 0 ≤ x < 1, and n > 0, the derivative is positive. Increasing ρ increases the score. That directly contradicts the stated principle that increasing contradiction density should reduce rigor or certainty.

04 / REAL-WORLD GATE

Every principle must pass five independent gates.

  1. Semantic: variables map to measurable quantities without circular definitions.
  2. Mathematical: domains, units, invariants, limits, and counterexamples are explicit.
  3. Computational: reference code, property tests, and independent implementations agree.
  4. Empirical: preregistered datasets and baselines can falsify the prediction.
  5. Formal: Lean proves exact propositions without placeholders, axioms, or floating-point substitution.
